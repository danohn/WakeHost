WakeHost Privacy Policy

update Last updated: March 12, 2026

WakeHost is built on a foundation of local-first privacy. We believe your network infrastructure should be your business alone. This policy outlines our commitment to transparency and the limited scope of data required to make WakeHost work for you.

Information WakeHost uses

To provide its core functionality, WakeHost processes information related to your local network environment. This includes:

  • router Host identifiers (MAC addresses) for the devices you wish to wake.
  • dns IP addresses and hostnames within your local area network (LAN).
  • settings_ethernet Network interface details provided by macOS to establish local connectivity.

How information is stored

storage

Local-Only Storage

All configuration data, including host lists and wake schedules, is stored exclusively on your local machine. WakeHost utilizes standard macOS application support directories.

cloud_off

No Cloud Sync

We do not provide cloud synchronization services. Your data never leaves your device unless you manually export it or use third-party backup tools.

How information is used

Information is used solely to execute the "Wake-on-LAN" (WoL) protocol. When you trigger a wake event or a schedule occurs, the application uses the stored MAC address to broadcast a "magic packet" over your local network.

Data sharing

"WakeHost does not sell, trade, or share your local network data with third parties. Period."

Since your data is not collected on our servers, there is no dataset to share. We do not integrate with third-party advertising or data brokerage services.

Analytics and tracking

visibility_off

The application is designed to work locally and does not include analytics or third-party tracking pixels.

We do not monitor how you use the app or how many devices you manage.

Local network access

On modern versions of macOS, WakeHost will explicitly request permission to "Find and connect to devices on your local network." This is a native macOS privacy feature. If denied, WakeHost will be unable to send magic packets to other devices on your LAN.
